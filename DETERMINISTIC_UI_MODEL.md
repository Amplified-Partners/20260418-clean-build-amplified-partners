# The Deterministic UI Model

Design is not art. It is deterministic engineering. We do not guess aesthetics in one hop; we build a Polish Engine that iterates based on hard data, exactly as enterprise UI teams do.

## 🧠 Step 1: The Baseline (Cognitive Load)
We do not invent new layouts. We stand on the shoulders of giants. The dashboards produced by legacy SaaS (Salesforce, etc.) might look terrible, but they are brilliant underneath because they *work*. They have spent billions optimizing for cognitive load and behavioral psychology. We strip their visuals but steal their underlying deterministic framework for layout and hierarchy.

## 🗣️ Step 2: The Human Feedback Loop
We research raw internet feedback. We scrape the complaints and praises of human users regarding complex interfaces. We extract the exact words they use to describe what is important and what is noise. This becomes the constraint filter. 

## ⚙️ Step 3: The Polish Engine (Iterative Synthesis)
We do not ask the AI to "make it look nice." We feed the machine:
1. The Cognitive Baseline (Step 1).
2. The Human Constraints (Step 2).
3. Aesthetic Reference Points (The "nice looking things").

The machine then iterates over the interface. It isn't guessing; it is balancing the cognitive load equation against the human feedback loop. The aesthetic output is a byproduct of pure, technical determinism.
