# The "Give Away The Show" Walkthrough (Marketing Funnel)

This is not a traditional SaaS website. It is an interactive, linear progression that proves the math and the utility of Amplified Partners before the client ever speaks to a human.

## Stage 1: The Contextualizer
- **Prompt:** "Would you like to see how the product works in reality?"
- **Action:** Ask a simple question to contextualize the demo (e.g., "What are your main responsibilities?"). 
- **Rule:** If they don't want to answer, the system defaults to a generalized example. No friction.

## Stage 2: The Voice-First Revelation
- **Visual:** A plain screen. No buttons. No menus.
- **Copy:** "No buttons to press. Nothing to learn. You talk to it, and it does what you say."
- **Demonstration:** Show (don't tell) that administrative tasks (emails, paying invoices) happen automatically. We do not use cheesy examples like "Email Mrs. Smith." We use brutal reality: "Invoice reconciled. Email sent."

## Stage 3: The Live Telephone Test
- **Prompt:** "Do you want to try the telephone speech?"
- **Action:** A real, live AI voice session begins right in the browser. 
- **The Voice:** Not overly chatty. Professional, utilitarian. *"I am the Amplified Partners AI assistant. How can I help?"*
- **The Proof:** As the user speaks, the text flows underneath in real-time so they can visually verify the exact accuracy. 
- **The Close:** *"Did I capture that accurately?"*

## Stage 4: The Staff Experience
- **Visual:** The Staff Interface. Extremely plain.
- **The Logic:** Staff only see 3 tasks at a time. *"What are my tasks today?"* They do them. They disappear. If they want more, it refills. 
- **The Hook:** Subtle gamification (the "farting haggis" streak tracker) to maintain morale and engagement without adding complex dashboards.

## Stage 5: The Business Brain (Animation)
- **Visual:** A clean, unpretentious animation showing how data flows.
- **Copy:** "We take all of the information you allow us to take, feed it into your business brain, and layer it on top of a best-practice framework."
- **Reassurance:** "Best practice does not mean changing the way you run your business. It means giving you the statistical guidelines that prove what works."

## Stage 6: The Final Interview (The Close)
- **The Offer:** We don't ask for a credit card. We ask for data. 
- **Copy:** "If you give us access to your data, and you do the interview, this is exactly what we can do for you."
- **Action:** The client transitions directly into the 3-to-10 question Voice Interview to begin onboarding.
