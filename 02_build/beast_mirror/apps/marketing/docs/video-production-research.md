## 2. RECORDING EWAN'S CUSTOM AVATAR

This is the foundation. A bad training video means a bad avatar forever. Get this right once and everything downstream is better.

### HeyGen Avatar IV (Digital Twin)

HeyGen's Avatar IV is the current state of the art. It analyses vocal tone, rhythm, and emotion to generate photorealistic facial movements — including head tilts, natural pauses, micro-expressions, and timing-aware hand gestures that match what you're actually saying.

**Recording requirements:**

- **Camera:** 4K camera or modern smartphone in cinematic mode at 4K
- **Duration:** 2.5 to 3 minutes continuous (minimum 2 minutes, up to 5 for best training results)
- **ONE TAKE:** Do not edit or splice. One continuous recording.
- **Distance:** 2-3 feet from camera
- **Framing:** Head and shoulders centred in frame
- **Camera angle:** Eye level. No tilting. No looking down at a laptop camera.
- **Focus:** Disable auto-focus. Use manual focus locked on face.
- **Exposure:** Lock exposure by tapping and holding on your face until the lock icon appears.

**Lighting:**

- Face a window with soft, indirect sunlight. Not direct sun — that creates harsh shadows.
- Two diffused lights on either side works if no window available.
- Even lighting on the background too. No dramatic shadows.
- Avoid backlight (window behind you = silhouette = useless).

**Background:**

- Simple, clean, distraction-free. A plain wall works.
- Good contrast between you and the background.
- No busy patterns, no moving elements, no other people visible.
- If you plan to replace the background later, solid colour is best.

**What to wear:**

- Solid colours. No busy patterns, especially near face and neck.
- No reflective glasses if possible. If wearing glasses, position lights to minimise reflections.
- Whatever Ewan would actually wear. Not a suit if he doesn't wear suits. Authenticity starts here.

**Performance during recording:**

- Speak at a natural, moderate pace. Like you're explaining something to a mate.
- Natural facial expressions — not exaggerated, not frozen.
- Steady eye contact with the camera lens. Don't let eyes drift.
- First 15 seconds are critical: minimise blinking and excessive movement.
- Subtle hand gestures only. Keep hands closed with occasional emphasis, then return to neutral anchor position.
- Avoid quick or jerky movements. Limit head turns to 30 degrees.
- Keep hands away from face/mouth.

**Audio:**

- Quiet room. No background noise, no other voices.
- No music. Just Ewan talking.

### Synthesia Personal Avatar

Similar principles, fewer specific technical requirements:

- Single continuous video, no splicing
- Well-lit face, simple background
- Solid colours for clothing
- Both eyes visible at all times
- Speak clearly and naturally
- No exaggerated expressions (causes distortions)
- Hands away from mouth
- No face coverings

**Key difference:** Synthesia's personal avatar is quicker to set up (webcam recording works) but less realistic than HeyGen's Avatar IV. HeyGen is the social content workhorse; Synthesia is the training/onboarding tool.

## 3. SCRIPTING FOR SPOKEN WORD (Not Written English)

This is where 90% of AI avatar videos fail. People write blog posts and feed them into a video tool. The avatar reads an essay. It sounds wrong because it IS wrong — written English and spoken English are different languages.

### The Rules

**1. Write for the ear, not the eye.**

Bad: "Small businesses face significant challenges in maintaining compliance with evolving regulatory frameworks whilst simultaneously delivering exceptional customer experiences."

Good: "Look, you know the drill. There's a new form to fill in. There's a new rule you didn't know about. And your customer's standing there wanting their boiler fixed. You can't do both at the same time."

**2. Use contractions. Always.**

- "You're" not "you are"
- "It's" not "it is"
- "Can't" not "cannot"
- "Don't" not "do not"

If it sounds like a legal document, rewrite it.

**3. Short sentences. Then a long one. Then short again.**

Rhythm matters. Monotone sentence length puts people to sleep. Vary it. Let the short ones punch. Let the longer ones breathe and explain. Then punch again.

**4. The Two-Second Rule**

After every key point, insert a 2-second pause. This gives the viewer time to absorb what was just said. In HeyGen, use punctuation and line breaks to create these pauses. Full stop. New line. The AI reads the pause.

**5. Punctuation controls pacing.**

Commas = short breath.
Full stops = natural pause.
Ellipsis (...) = thinking pause.
Em dash — = change of direction.

Use them deliberately. The AI reads punctuation as timing cues.

**6. One idea per segment.**

Each 15-20 second chunk should make ONE point. Not three. Not two. One. Then move on. The viewer's attention is a gift — don't waste it by cramming.

**7. Mark emphasis words.**

In HeyGen's editor, mark keywords that need vocal emphasis — benefits, numbers, names, calls to action. The AI will stress these words in delivery.

**8. Talk TO someone, not AT an audience.**

Bad: "Business owners should consider implementing automated compliance checking."

Good: "You've got a compliance check due next week. What if it was already done? What if it just... happened?"

The script should feel like one person talking to one other person.

## 4. THE AMPLIFIED VIDEO STYLE GUIDE

### What We're Going For

- **Real over polished.** A plumber explaining a common problem beats a corporate presenter reading stats.
- **Empathy over authority.** "I know this is hard" beats "You should be doing X."
- **Specific over general.** "That file you need to write but it gets lost because you're too busy" beats "administrative burden."
- **Questions over statements.** "What if that just happened automatically?" hooks better than "Our system automates compliance."

### What We're NOT Going For

- Corporate training voice. Ever.
- Buzzwords: "leverage," "synergise," "optimise," "scalable solutions."
- The hustle bro tone. No "CRUSHING IT" energy.
- Pity. These people aren't victims. They're skilled professionals weighed down by admin.
- Overselling. We don't promise miracles. We promise less friction.

### The Ewan Voice in Video

Ewan talks like someone who's been there. 30 years running SMBs. He knows the 3am worry about cashflow. He knows the frustration of compliance paperwork. He knows the pride of building something with your hands.

The video scripts should sound like Ewan sitting across from you at a pub, saying: "Right, here's the thing nobody tells you..."

Specific Ewan-isms to capture:
- Direct address ("you," "your," never "one" or "businesses")
- North East flavour without forcing it
- Humour that comes from recognition, not jokes
- Zero corporate speak
- Swearing where it's natural (the odd "bloody" or "bollocks" is fine)
- Short declarative sentences when making a point
- Questions to pull the viewer in

## 5. PRODUCTION CHECKLIST

### Before Recording Training Video

- [ ] 4K camera/phone ready, cinematic mode
- [ ] Manual focus locked on face
- [ ] Exposure locked
- [ ] Camera at eye level, not tilted
- [ ] Soft lighting from front, no backlighting
- [ ] Simple background, good contrast
- [ ] Solid colour clothing, no busy patterns
- [ ] Quiet room, no background noise
- [ ] Script or talking points ready (but NOT reading from a script — know the material)
- [ ] 2.5-3 minute recording planned

### Before Publishing Any Video

- [ ] Script sounds like spoken word, not written English
- [ ] Contractions used throughout
- [ ] One idea per segment (15-20 seconds)
- [ ] 2-second pauses after key points
- [ ] Emphasis marked on keywords
- [ ] Talking TO one person, not AT an audience
- [ ] No corporate speak, no buzzwords
- [ ] Empathy shown for the viewer's situation
- [ ] Specific examples used, not vague generalities
- [ ] CTA is clear and simple
- [ ] Minimum 1080p resolution
- [ ] Lip sync verified
- [ ] Audio levels checked
- [ ] Watched with sound off (does it still make sense visually?)

## 6. PLATFORM-SPECIFIC VIDEO GUIDES

### HeyGen Social Clips (60-90 seconds)

**Structure:**
1. **Hook (0-5s):** Question or bold statement that makes someone stop scrolling. "You know that file you keep losing?"
2. **Problem (5-20s):** Name the specific pain. Make them nod.
3. **Insight (20-50s):** The thing they didn't know, or the reframe that changes how they see it.
4. **Takeaway (50-70s):** One actionable thing they can do right now. Value delivered.
5. **Soft CTA (70-90s):** "If this is your life every Tuesday, we should talk." Not "BUY NOW."

**Production tips:**
- Add subtle background music that SUPPORTS, doesn't compete. Low volume.
- Cut to B-roll or graphics every 10-20 seconds to maintain visual interest.
- Use title cards for key stats or quotes — boosts engagement 30-40%.
- 1-2 gestures per minute maximum. More than that looks manic.

### Synthesia Training/Onboarding (2-5 minutes)

**Structure:**
1. **Context (0-15s):** "Here's what we're covering and why it matters to you."
2. **Steps (15s-4m):** Clear, numbered steps. One concept per section. Visuals for each step.
3. **Summary (last 30s):** "Three things to remember." Recap the essentials.
4. **Next step:** "If you need help with this, here's where to go."

**Production tips:**
- More polished than HeyGen clips — this is reference material people will watch more than once.
- Include on-screen text for key points.
- Use consistent branding (colours, fonts, logo placement).
- Multilingual support available — relevant for Amplified's growth.
- Keep each section under 60 seconds. People can pause and rewatch, but they won't watch a 5-minute monologue.

## 7. THE AMPLIFIED DIFFERENCE

Everyone else using HeyGen/Synthesia is doing one of two things:

1. **Corporate training** — boring, stiff, avatar reads a script. Works but doesn't connect.
2. **Social spam** — avatar reads a blog post word for word. Gets views, delivers nothing.

What Amplified does differently:

1. **Research-grounded scripts** — The script addresses a pain point the research agent already identified. Not "what should we talk about" but "this is what 47 plumbers asked about on Reddit this week."

2. **Human-AI scripting** — Ewan writes the emotional core, the lived experience, the "I've been there." Claude structures it, tightens the pacing, adds the data points. Neither could do it as well alone.

3. **Enforcer validation** — Every video script runs through tone, quality, and compliance rubrics before it touches HeyGen or Synthesia. No AI slop gets through.

4. **Platform-native** — A HeyGen social clip is structured differently from a Synthesia training video. They serve different purposes, different audiences, different moments.

5. **Voice fidelity** — The scripts capture Ewan's actual voice and personality. Dave at Jesmond Plumbing gets scripts that sound like Dave. Not like a marketing agency pretending to be Dave.

6. **Feedback loop** — Engagement data on every published video feeds back into FalkorDB. What topics performed? What hooks worked? What fell flat? The next cycle's scripts are informed by real data, not guesses.
