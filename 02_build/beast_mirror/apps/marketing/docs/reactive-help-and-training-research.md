## The Concept: Reactive Help

Traditional SaaS sends you a newsletter when you don't log in. We do the opposite.

When someone in our system signals they're struggling — a frustrated message, a drop in engagement, a direct "I've had a terrible day" — we don't try to sell them anything. We send them something genuinely useful. A 15-minute video from one of the best communicators on earth. A podcast episode that speaks directly to what they're dealing with. A framework they can use tomorrow.

**This is the system:**
1. **Detect the signal** — sentiment analysis on interactions, direct statements, engagement drop-off
2. **Match the problem** — difficult client? Staff issues? Overwhelm? Cash flow stress? Loneliness?
3. **Serve the resource** — curated, tested, brilliant content from the best in the world
4. **No strings attached** — no "and by the way, upgrade your plan." Just help.

The flywheel: they remember we helped them on their worst day. That's worth more than any ad campaign ever built.

## Implementation: How This Becomes a System

### Phase 1: Curated Library (Now)
- Build the resource database in FalkorDB
- Tag every resource by: problem_type, duration, format, cost, intensity, trade_relevance
- Quality-test every link (is it still live? Still good?)
- Write the "what we'd send" message for each

### Phase 2: Sentiment Detection (Month 2-3)
- Monitor interaction sentiment through the platform
- Key signals: negative language, frustration indicators, engagement drop-off, direct statements
- NOT invasive — based on what they tell us, not surveillance

### Phase 3: Reactive Matching (Month 3-4)
- When signal detected → match to resource category → select best resource
- Personalise by DISC profile (a Dominant personality gets Chris Voss; a Steady personality gets Brené Brown)
- Send via their preferred channel (email, platform notification, SMS if opted in)

### Phase 4: Feedback & Improvement (Ongoing)
- Track: did they click? Did they come back? Did sentiment improve?
- Ask (optionally): "Was that helpful?" — simple thumbs up/down
- Feed results back into FalkorDB → improve matching over time

## What We'd NEVER Do

1. Use someone's bad day to sell them an upgrade
2. Pretend we created the content — always credit the source
3. Send unsolicited "are you okay?" messages based on surveillance
4. Gate any of these resources behind a paywall or login
5. Track emotional data for any purpose other than serving better help
6. Share anyone's emotional signals with third parties, ever
