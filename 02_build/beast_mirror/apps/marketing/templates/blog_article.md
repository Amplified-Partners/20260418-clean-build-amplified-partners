# Blog Article Template

## Structure

```markdown
# [Title — Include Main Keyword Naturally]

[Opening paragraph — Hook the reader with a relatable problem or question.
Set up what they'll learn. 2-3 sentences.]

## [Subheading 1 — The Problem or Context]

[2-3 paragraphs explaining the issue. Use real-world examples.
Keep it conversational — write like you're explaining to a neighbour.]

## [Subheading 2 — Practical Tips / Solutions]

[The meat of the article. 3-5 actionable tips.
Each tip should be something the reader can actually do.]

## [Subheading 3 — When to Call a Professional]

[Honest assessment of when DIY isn't enough.
This is where the soft CTA lives — but it's genuinely helpful.]

## [Closing / Summary]

[Brief wrap-up. Reinforce the key takeaway.
Soft CTA: "If you need a hand..." not "CALL NOW!"]
```

## Requirements
- 500-800 words
- Main keyword appears naturally 3-5 times (no stuffing)
- At least 2 subheadings for structure
- At least 3 practical, actionable tips
- Honest — include DIY solutions even if it means less business
- Local references where they fit naturally
- No jargon without explanation
- End with soft CTA

## SEO Checklist
- [ ] Title includes main keyword
- [ ] Title under 60 characters
- [ ] Meta description under 160 characters
- [ ] Main keyword in first paragraph
- [ ] Subheadings include related keywords
- [ ] Internal links to services page (when published on client website)
- [ ] Reads naturally — SEO never overrides readability

## Tone
- Knowledgeable but not condescending
- Genuinely helpful — give away the knowledge
- "Here's what I'd tell my mum" energy
- Local pride without being parochial
- Professional but warm
