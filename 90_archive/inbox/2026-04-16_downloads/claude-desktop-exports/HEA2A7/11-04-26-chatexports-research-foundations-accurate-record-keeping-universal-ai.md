# Research Foundations for Accurate Record Keeping

## What Happened

User asked Claude to research how to accurately reflect a document without personal bias or interpretation. User framed this as "standing on the shoulders of giants" — finding established disciplines that had already solved this problem. Claude searched across journalism, archival science, and summarization research.

User then uploaded three articles: a reflexivity in research paper (Olmos-Vega et al., 2022), a biased writing guide (reporterist.com), and a research integrity paper on cognitive biases in secondary data analysis. User asked whether these added to the methodology Claude had found.

Claude identified three additions: reflexivity as a framework for the feedback loop (four dimensions — personal, interpersonal, methodological, contextual), three cognitive biases to guard against (confirmation bias, apophenia, hindsight bias), and a loaded-words checklist as a measurable validation step.

User pushed back on whether the four reflexivity dimensions were practical for an AI. Claude mapped them to AI equivalents: training data bias, positional bias, first-impression colouring, and context leaking. User agreed the dimensions should be named as awareness but said the loaded-words check was the measurable part.

## Decisions Made

- Three disciplines provide the methodological foundation: journalism, archival science, summarization research
- Journalism (Kovach and Rosenstiel): the method is objective, not the journalist
- Archival science: provenance (keep records from different origins separate) and original order (maintain the sequence the creator established)
- Summarization research: summaries must be fair, consistent with source, free of tonal or ideological leanings
- Reflexivity provides the framework for the feedback loop — four named dimensions
- Three cognitive biases to guard against: confirmation bias, apophenia, hindsight bias
- Loaded-words check is measurable: compare every adjective/adverb in output against the source
- Two layers in the skill: measurable validation (loaded words) and reflexive guidance (four dimensions)

## Sources

- Kovach and Rosenstiel, The Elements of Journalism — "the method is objective, not the journalist"
- Hilary Jenkinson (1937) — "the physical and moral defense of the record"
- Dutch Manual (1898) — original order principle for archival arrangement
- US National Archives (Schellenberg, 1951) — provenance protects integrity of records
- Olmos-Vega et al. (2022), AMEE Guide No. 149 — reflexivity as continuous, collaborative, multifaceted practice
- reporterist.com — loaded words checklist: avoid "unfortunately," "clearly," "obviously," "surprisingly"
- BMC research integrity paper — confirmation bias, apophenia, hindsight bias in secondary data analysis
- Abstractive summarization survey — fair, accurate, consistent, resilient to input noise
